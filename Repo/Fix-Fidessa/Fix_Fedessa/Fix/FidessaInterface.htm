Sample Order flow process in Fidessa:

 

Client can place 2 types of orders.

1) manual Orders – Orders placed by any other means of communication other than through OMS system.

2) Electronic Orders – Orders placed through Client OMS system.

Scenario : Say suppose Client A want to place a limit order for 70000 shares of HSBC (Stock Code – 5.HK) through Hong Kong exchange @market price. Assume Order placed through the client OMS system.

 

a)      The first entry of the order comes to the inbox of the Fidessa Trader workbench (FTW).

 

 

 

 

 

 

b)      The order is picked by the Sales Trader by selecting the respective order and clicking on the Accept Button. The order is then owned by the Sales trader and status of the order is changed to OPEN state.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

c)      Once the order is owned by the sales trader can route the order to the dealer or for himself. If the sales trader wants to work on that order he routes for himself else if he wants to divert to the ownership to the dealer he sends it to the primary Trader (Dealer).

 

Routing to him:

 

 

 

Routing to Dealer:

 

d)      In our example let’s consider the sales trader wants to work on the order and thereby he routes the order to himself. Once the order is routed to him you can see the state of order changed from OPEN to ASSIGNED. Under the MS Order column you can see the Owner name as sales trader himself.

e)      Once the order is assigned to him the sales trader can fill the order either by “House Fill” or “Broker Fill”.

MSLMKT is one of the House Book used by Macquarie. “House Fill” will be transferred out of this book to fill the client Order.

“Broker Fill” is done by one of the 3rd party broker to complete the client order fill.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

House Fill:

 

Clicking on the House fill opens a House fill window from which the user can fill the client order.

 

Broker Fill:

 

Clicking on the Broker fill opens a Broker fill window from which the user can send the order to a particular 3rd party broker.

In the below screen the Counterparty refers to the 3rd party broker to whom the order is diverted for the fill.

 

f)        In our case let’s use the House fill to fill the client order. Right click on the order and select the “House fill” sub-menu under the “Fill” menu. Enter the quantity you want to House Fill and click the Fill button. Once the order has been fully filled you can see MS Order column value as “Filled” for the corresponding order.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

g)      In our case as the order is completely filled the sales trader can complete the order and Book it. In case the order is partially filled and the user wants to work more on the partially filled order, he can just book for the quantity that is partially filled. And if he doesn’t wants to work anymore on the partially filled order he can complete the order and book it. The unfilled quantity will be cancelled in that case.

 

Complete and Book:

 

Once the user clicks on the complete menu, the state of the order changes from Assigned to Unbooked. Click on the unbooked tab to view the Order.

 

Select the order under Unbooked tab and right-click to Book it.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

User will be prompted with a booking order window where he needs to click on the Book button to complete the booking.

 

 

 

 

 

Once the order is successfully booked the state of the order changes from Unbooked to Fully Booked.

 

And finally the order details are forwarded to Middle Office for further processing.
